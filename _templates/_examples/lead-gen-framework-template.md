## The 5-Module Lead Gen Blueprint

### MODULE 1 — Value Proposition
**Teaching:** Before publishing anything, communicate value in one clear sentence — not what you do, but what the client *gets*. If a stranger can't understand the offering in 10 seconds after visiting the profile, the brand is invisible.

**Gap to close:** The value proposition lives in the strategy deliverable but may never make it into the content.

**Implementation:**
- Add a "Value Prop Translation" step to client onboarding: distill the brand promise to one [PRIMARY_CHANNEL]-ready sentence.
- Every post ladders back to this sentence — explicitly or implicitly.
- This becomes the filter: if a post doesn't reinforce the value prop, it doesn't go live.

---

### MODULE 2 — Profile Optimization (The Virtual Storefront)
**Teaching:** The profile is a sales page, not a resume. Every section — headline, banner, about, featured — answers one question: *why should my ideal client trust me and reach out?* If the profile doesn't convert cold visitors, content is driving traffic to a broken storefront.

**Gap to close:** Profile work may be limited to higher tiers, leaving lower-tier clients posting to an unconverted profile.

**Implementation:**
- Add a **Profile Setup Sprint** to all tiers as a one-time onboarding deliverable (first 2 weeks, before posting begins).
- Components: headline rewrite, banner design, about section rewrite, featured section setup, skills/endorsements audit.
- Price into onboarding fee, or as an add-on at `[PROFILE_SPRINT_PRICE]` one-time.
- For higher tiers: ongoing optimization (A/B headline testing, updated featured section quarterly).

---

### MODULE 3 — Content Blueprint (3x/Week, Optimized for Sales)
**Teaching:** Post 3 times a week with intention. Each post serves a strategic purpose: authority, relatability, or conversion. The content calendar is about sequencing, not frequency.

**Gap to close:** Posts may exist but lack strategic sequencing across the three content types.

**Content Type Breakdown:**
| Type | Purpose | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Authority | Proves expertise, builds credibility | "3 things I learned after [X client engagements]" |
| Relatability | Builds trust, humanizes the brand | "I almost made this mistake. Here's what I caught." |
| Conversion | Plants a seed for the offer | "If your [channel] profile hasn't changed since [year], your buyers notice." |

**Implementation:**
- Build a rotating 3-post sequence into the content calendar template.
- Every month = 4 cycles (12 posts) with intentional sequencing.
- Add hooks that stop the scroll: open loops, bold claims, story-first structure.

---

### MODULE 4 — Network Growth + DM Strategy (THE REVENUE DRIVER)
**Teaching:** Content gets visibility. Network growth gets in front of buyers. DMs close deals. If only doing content, you're farming without harvesting. The DM strategy is warm follow-through — when someone engages with a post, that's an invitation.

**Gap to close:** This module is commonly absent from all tiers. The single biggest gap in most content operations.

**Implementation Options:**

**Option A — Include in higher tiers:**
- Weekly connection outreach to 20–30 ICP-targeted profiles.
- Engagement responses on comments from authority figures in client's niche.
- DM templates for warm follow-through (when someone engages → send non-spammy value add).

**Option B — Offer as an Add-On (`[ADDON_NAME]` at `[ADDON_PRICE]`):**
- Positions as the "harvesting" layer on top of the "farming" layer.
- Gives clients a clear upgrade path when ready to convert visibility into conversations.

*Recommended: Start as add-on to test demand and define workflow, then roll into higher tiers once documented.*

---

### MODULE 5 — The Full System (Compounding)
**Teaching:** This is where everything compounds. Profile converts cold traffic. Content builds warm audiences. Network growth surfaces buyers. DMs convert them. When all five run together, [PRIMARY_CHANNEL] becomes a primary revenue source — without ads, without chasing.

**Implementation:**
- The top tier (`[TIER_3_NAME]`) is the only tier where all 5 modules are active simultaneously.
- Position top tier as the compounding tier — where the [PRIMARY_CHANNEL] machine is fully built AND fueled.
- Use language: "This is where [PRIMARY_CHANNEL] stops being a job and starts being an asset."

## Key Philosophy Shifts

### 1. Lead with what you're replacing
Open with what's NOT working (mass posting, cold DMs, paid ads). Reframe the offering: *"Most agencies write content. We build a lead generation system."*

### 2. The profile is the storefront — always
No client goes live without a profile that converts. Non-negotiable.

### 3. Quality over frequency, sequence over volume
3 posts/week in the right sequence beats 7 random posts. Sell this as a feature, not a limitation.

### 4. Peace over pressure
Premium clients come from a strategic system, not from hustle. Set expectations accordingly.

### 5. [PRIMARY_CHANNEL] is a long game with compounding returns
Results come from consistency + system, not viral moments. Set client expectations accordingly.
